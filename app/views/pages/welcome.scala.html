Do you need to register for VAT?

You need to register for VAT if you expect your company's VAT taxable turnover to be more than £83,000 in the 30 days after it's registered.

Can you register voluntarily for VAT?

You can register voluntarily if your company's VAT taxable turnover is less than £83,000, unless everything you sell is exempt from VAT.

If your company is registered for VAT you:

  • can charge VAT on the goods or services you sell
  • can reclaim VAT on your business-related purchases

VAT-registered companies can appear larger and more established to customers. However, customers that aren't VAT registered (and can't reclaim VAT) may be put off by the increased prices that come with VAT registration.

You can cancel your VAT registration at any time if it turns out to be the wrong decision.

What are your VAT responsibilities?

From the effective date of registration you must:

  • charge the right amount of VAT
  • pay any VAT due to HMRC
  • submit VAT Returns
  • keep VAT records and a VAT account
